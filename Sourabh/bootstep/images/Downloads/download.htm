GIF vs Animated PNG

While everyone is busy arguing over the pronunciation of a 30+ year old image format, the Animated PNG has gone under the radar for over a decade. But which is better (GIF vs Animated PNG)? The APNG is much superior when compared to GIF. To prove this, please look at the examples below.

So when it comes to GIF vs Animated PNG, the APNG not only looks better (it supports 24-bit images and 8-bit transparency), but is actually smaller in size! Why aren’t we using these? ðŸ¤”

To make your own APNG, download APNG Assembler. You’ll need to render out a PNG sequence to make an APNG. I used a 1/24 frame delay for my 24 fps walk cycle. Open the APNG in a browser that is compatible. Well, which browsers support APNG? Here’s a browser compatibility report. If it isn’t supported, the animated PNG will essentially become a still PNG. You can read more on the official wiki.

Currently, only a handful of platforms support APNG. Imgur supports APNGs, and Dribbble accepts animated PNGs via attachments. If you know of any other platforms that also are compatible, please let me know!
